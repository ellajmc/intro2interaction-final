Anthony Squires Menswear

David Mist orchestrated a visually captivating display, showcasing his remarkable images against the iconic backdrop of Uluru in the Northern Territory desert. Our collaboration brought together the artistry of Mist's photography with Visuell's expertise in advertising, culminating in a celebration of Anthony Squires Menswear's 21-year legacy through striking imagery and creative vision.

Flair Magazine

Mist's photographic work was also a prominent feature among many magazines in Australia. Our team curated fashion editorial layouts designed to feature his incredible work in magazines such as Flair Magazine (pictured) in the late '60s and early '70s, Women's Weekly in the '80s, as well as Westfield Shoppingtown catalogues in the late 80's and early '90s.

David Jones

In a mesmerizing collaboration, David Mist Photography teamed up with Visuell to curate an unforgettable photoshoot for David Jones, a pivotal part of Vogue Australia's Swimwear Story in the March 1977 issue. Set against the breathtaking backdrop of Dunk Island in Queensland, the images captured the essence of sun-soaked glamour and tropical allure. Seamlessly blending Mist's keen eye for composition and Visuell's innovative display techniques, the collaboration brought to life an exquisite visual narrative, showcasing the latest swimwear trends amidst the pristine beauty of Australia's coastline.

Commercial Photography Archive

Delving into David Mist's extensive photographic archive offers a compelling journey through decades and across continents. From personal snapshots to exhibition pieces, commercial endeavors to iconic fashion shoots, Mist's portfolio showcases his versatile talent and artistic vision. The collection encompasses various mediums, including prints, color, and monochrome images, transparencies, and negatives, reflecting Mist's adeptness in the craft. Within the archive lie notable works such as ' Sydney: a book of photographs' and 'Made in Australia,' both published by Paul Hamlyn in 1969. Additionally, materials from Mist's Australian Centre for Photography exhibition titled 'Mystery' in 1978 provide insight into his exploration of enigmatic themes.

Alongside professional endeavors, the archive offers glimpses into Mist's personal life, with biographical materials, portraits, and encounters with notable figures like photographer Laurence Le Guay providing context. Spanning diverse locales from London and Sydney to Europe, the USA, Asia, and beyond, Mist's photography captures the essence of varied landscapes and human experiences. Through his lens, viewers embark on a visual journey that traverses time and space, offering a nuanced perspective on the world around us.

Hickory Underwear

David Mist's collaboration with the W.B. Lawrence advertising agency for Hickory Underwear stands as a testament to his creative prowess and keen eye for capturing iconic moments. Teaming up with Australian model Lyndal Moore, Mist crafted captivating imagery that celebrated mankind's historic landing on the moon on 26 July 1969. Tasked with showcasing the essence of the "luna look" and embracing the space-age fashion trend that had been gaining traction, Mist and the agency embarked on a visionary journey.

The resulting imagery, featuring Moore adorned in Hickory Underwear garments, exudes a sense of cosmic wonder and futuristic allure. Through meticulous attention to detail and seamless collaboration with the creative team, Visuell brought the agency's vision to life, creating a visual narrative that transcends time and space. The space-themed image serves as a captivating homage to a pivotal moment in history, while also showcasing the timeless elegance of Hickory Underwear's designs.

Accent Shoes

David Mist's photography for Accent Shoes were prominently featured as advertisements in publications like Vogue magazine as well as on posters, billboards throughout Sydney. Visuell contributed to the Accent Shoes project as a visionary body, ensuring the brand's messaging was communicated in the photography, showcasing their exquisite footwear and the essence of the late '70s fashion landscape, creating iconic pieces of visual storytelling.

Pierre Cardin

Visuell was asked to take part in a collaboration with Pierre Cardin for their 1983 Paris photoshoot. We brought our trustworthy photographer, David Mist, to ensure the imagery would be a global success. Mist's photgraphs showcase iconic landmarks such as the Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe, and the serene Seine River. This captivating series captured not only the essence of Pierre Cardin's designs but also the timeless allure of the city of Paris itself, blending fashion and culture seamlessly in Mist's signature style.
